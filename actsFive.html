Acts: Chapter Five

Overview

The early Church faces internal challenges, as the story of Ananias and Sapphira reveals. Their deception in withholding part of their offering is met with severe judgment, underscoring the importance of integrity and sincerity within the Christian community.

Following this, the apostles are imprisoned for preaching, but an angel frees them, and they continue proclaiming the Gospel with even greater boldness.

-Insite-

This chapter shows the seriousness with which God views integrity in the Church. The dramatic judgment of Ananias and Sapphira serves as a warning against hypocrisy.

Meanwhile, the miraculous release of the apostles from prison demonstrates that no earthly power can prevent the spread of the Gospel.
Their relentless preaching, despite opposition, highlights the unstoppable nature of the early Church’s mission.

Acts: Chapter Five

Summary

Ananias and Sapphira lie about their offering to the church and face divine judgment.
Meanwhile, the apostles perform many signs and wonders, but they are imprisoned by the religious leaders. An angel frees them, and they continue preaching the gospel.

Themes

• Integrity in the Church
• God’s Judgment and Mercy
• Apostolic Signs and Wonders
