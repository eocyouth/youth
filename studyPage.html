Bible Study

Acts of the Apostles

Welcome to our collection of study notes on the first 10 chapters of the Book of Acts. Here, you’ll find comprehensive overviews,
detailed summaries, and key verses for each chapter. Our goal is to help you dive deeper into the Word and
understand the powerful stories and lessons from this foundational book of the Bible.


  • Acts: Chapter One

    This chapter begins the book of Acts with a continuation of the story of Jesus Christ after His resurrection.

  • Acts: Chapter Two

    On the Day of Pentecost, the Holy Spirit descends on the apostles, giving them the ability to speak in various tongues.

  • Acts: Chapter Three

    Peter and John’s healing of the lame beggar at the temple gate signifies the continuation of Jesus’ healing ministry through His apostles.

  • Acts: Chapter Four

    After healing the lame beggar, Peter and John are arrested and brought before the Sanhedrin, the Jewish religious council.

  • Acts: Chapter Five

    This chapter shows the seriousness with which God views integrity in the Church. The judgment of Ananias and Sapphira serves as a warning against hypocrisy.

  • Acts: Chapter Six

    Stephen, full of grace and power, performs signs and wonders, but his ministry stirs up opposition, leading to his arrest.

  • Acts: Chapter Seven

    Stephen delivers a powerful speech before the Sanhedrin, recounting Israel’s history and accusing the religious leaders of resisting the Holy Spirit

  • Acts: Chapter Eight

    After Stephen’s death, persecution forces many believers to flee Jerusalem. Philip preaches in Samaria, where many come to faith.

  • Acts: Chapter Nine

    Saul, a fierce persecutor of Christians, is dramatically converted on the road to Damascus. Blinded by a vision of Jesus, Saul is led to Damascus

  • Acts: Chapter Ten

    Cornelius, a Roman centurion, receives a vision instructing him to send for Peter. Meanwhile, Peter has a vision challenging Jewish dietary laws
